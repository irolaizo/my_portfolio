Journalism Final Project

Mass tourism under Francoism through cinema

Francoism through cinema

This final project focuses on Francoism as portrayed in cinema. I chose this topic because it seemed especially relevant to analyze the changes that took place during the dictatorship.

I approached the subject through media, particularly informative feature films. The core material for my study was the NO-DO newsreels — a key source for understanding the regime’s official narrative.

The work focuses on the 1960s, a decade marked by significant social and economic changes. In this context, mass tourism emerged as a major force driving growth.

From the 60s to today

Today, tourism remains a growing industry. What was once a rare opportunity has become a part of daily life, shaping the way we relate to the world.

Through family stories, we can reconstruct how that transition was experienced — from spontaneous trips to the first vacation memories.
