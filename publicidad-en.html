Analysis of dialect usage in Tokikom's local media

Dialects vs. Standard Basque

This study examines the use of Basque dialects (euskalkiak) and local linguistic variants in the Tokikom media group, compared to standard Basque (euskara batua).

50 print publications were analyzed (over 1200 pages), selected between January and May 2023, from different local outlets in Álava, Bizkaia, Gipuzkoa, and Navarra.

Surveys were also conducted with journalists and editorial leaders to explore their linguistic policies, views on dialect use, and editorial criteria.

Methodology & Findings

This mixed-method study combined discourse analysis and surveys. It found that standard Basque still dominates written media, but dialects play a key role in interviews and testimonies, adding local flavor and authenticity.

Media analyzed

  • Álava: Alea
  • Bizkaia: Aikor, Anboto, Geuria
  • Gipuzkoa: Ataria, Barren, Goiena
  • Navarra: Amezti-Pulunpe, Guaixe, Mailope

Conclusions

The study suggests promoting dialects more actively, without abandoning the standard. Future language policies could aim to balance identity with clarity, adapting norms to each region’s linguistic richness.
